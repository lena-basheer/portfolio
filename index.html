About

I don't just report numbers — I explain what they mean.

I'm a data analyst with a Computer Science foundation and a habit of asking "so what?" after every chart. My work spans the full analysis lifecycle: pulling messy, real-world data into shape with Python and SQL, exploring it until patterns surface, and packaging the findings into Power BI and Excel dashboards that non-technical stakeholders can actually use.

The thread running through my projects is the same one running through this site: take something unstructured — retail transactions, workforce records, even class timetables — and give it a clear, navigable shape. I've handled 500K+ transaction rows for a retail segmentation study, built HR dashboards for workforce analysis, and engineered a constraint-based scheduling engine as a software side project.

I'm currently looking for an entry-level Data Analyst role where I can turn that same instinct — clean it, question it, visualize it — into decisions a team can act on.
